Compassion (Jin)

Jin is the virtue of compassion and empathy. Samurai were expected to use their power not only for warfare but also for protecting and aiding those in need.

History & Cultural Significance

Jin emphasized benevolence as a hallmark of a true warrior. Samurai who acted with cruelty or indifference to the suffering of others were seen as dishonorable. Compassion balanced martial strength with humanity.

During times of peace, many samurai devoted themselves to governance, charity, and scholarship, extending their protective role into society.

Example in Practice

A samurai sparing the life of a surrendered opponent, or providing aid to a famine-stricken village, exemplifies Jin.

Practical Lesson

Compassion today means recognizing the struggles of others and acting to relieve suffering where possible. Strength without kindness is empty.

"Bushidō is realized in the presence of death. This means choosing death whenever there is a choice between life and death. There is no other reasoning." — Yamamoto Tsunetomo, Hagakure
