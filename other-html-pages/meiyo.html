Meiyo

Honor (Meiyo)

Meiyo is the virtue of honor, the esteem earned through righteous conduct. For a samurai, honor was worth more than life itself, as it defined their reputation and legacy.

History & Cultural Significance

Samurai culture placed immense importance on honor. A warrior who lost their honor might choose seppuku (ritual suicide) rather than live with disgrace. Honor shaped every action and ensured that warriors acted responsibly with the power they wielded.

Honor extended beyond the battlefield — in family life, governance, and commerce, a samurai’s word and reputation had to remain untarnished.

Example in Practice

Refusing bribes, keeping promises at great personal cost, or choosing death over betrayal were ways samurai upheld Meiyo.

Practical Lesson

Honor means aligning one’s actions with their values consistently. In modern life, this is the integrity of keeping your word and protecting your reputation through honesty.

"Bushidō is realized in the presence of death. This means choosing death whenever there is a choice between life and death. There is no other reasoning." — Yamamoto Tsunetomo, Hagakure
